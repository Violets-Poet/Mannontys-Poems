The Last Conversation with the Moon

November

"You cheated on me!" cried the moon, "I can't believe you cheated on me!"
"What did I do?"
"Even though you said it under the sun - when I wasn't there, when you thought I couldn't hear you - I saw you that night," sorrow filled its voice, "I put on my most dazzling stars for you, I cleared away all my clouds for you, and you still didn't - and you still couldn't take your eyes off of her!"

The moon paced around. Apparently, I had hurt it. Apparently, I was in the wrong? But I knew I wasn't. What did it expect?

"Oh, how many times have you told her, 'Isn't the moon pretty?' And every time, like a fool, I thought you were talking about me. Oh, how stupid of me. I should've seen the signs! I should've been jealous from the start!"
"Yes, you should have! You should always be jealous of her!" The words left my mouth before I could think. Not that I could think straight with her on my mind anyway. "She's perfect! Her beauty is perfect! Everything about her is perfect! How could anything not be jealous of her!"

The moon cried. I knew how it felt. It cried like the countless times I wanted to cry over her - like the one time I actually did. And just like me then, it couldn't stop crying. The air got heavy with its sad and cold tears, just like my heart did with mine back then.

"You said it, didn't you? You told her she's more beautiful than me?"
"She is more beautiful than you!"
"How could you?! How could you - you who never breaks a word - break your vow to me! You're just like the rest of them-"
"Do NOT compare me to them! I would never throw your name in vain. I would never run about and call every other girl beautiful, let alone more beautiful than you. But her... there's no one and nothing even remotely close to how beautiful and lovely she is. Not even you."

The moon sobbed. "Is she worth it? Is she worth the pain that I can clearly see you're in because of her? Is she worth you crying when I know nothing else can make you cry? Is she worth the time, energy, and mental space you give her? Is she worth the overflow of emotions? Even now as you're writing, I'm no longer 'Luna,' I'm just 'The moon.' I'm no longer 'her,' I'm not even a she, I'm an 'it!' Is she worth throwing away all the poems you wrote for me? All the poetry you said in my name? Throwing away our time together? Is she worth our relationship? Is she worth it?"

"She's worth it and so much more! She's worth more than everything I own and everything I am. She's worth living for, even if it means I'll be in constant pain and agony. As long as she's there, even if from afar, then a life, even in this pain, is better than death, even for me."

"She's worth it! She's worth it all! She's worth so much more! I love her! I love her more than I've ever loved you! I love her more than I thought I'm even capable of loving something or someone! I love her more than what should be possible! I love her!"

The moon refused to speak further. I don't think it will ever talk to me again.

I don't care.
I love her.
